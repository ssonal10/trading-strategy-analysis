üìà Key Performance Metrics

üéØ Alpha

Definition: The excess return generated by a strategy compared to simply buying and holding the stock.

Alpha = Strategy Total Return - Buy & Hold Return
Example: If TSLA + SMA_20 returned 2,620% and TSLA buy & hold returned 1,755%, then:
Alpha = 2,620% - 1,755% = +865% alpha

Interpretation: Positive alpha means the strategy beats buy & hold. Negative alpha means you should have just held the stock.

üí∞ Total Return

Definition: The total percentage gain/loss from the strategy over the entire 7-year period.

Total Return = (Final Portfolio Value - Initial Investment) / Initial Investment √ó 100%
Example: $10,000 invested in TSLA + SMA_20 strategy would have grown to $272,000, representing a 2,620% total return.

Note: This assumes reinvestment of all gains and 1% risk per trade with proper position sizing.

üé≤ Win Rate

Definition: The percentage of trades that were profitable.

Win Rate = (Number of Profitable Trades / Total Number of Trades) √ó 100%
Example: If a strategy made 72 trades and 39 were profitable:
Win Rate = 39/72 √ó 100% = 54.2%

Important: A strategy can be profitable with a win rate below 50% if winning trades are larger than losing trades.

üìä Trades

Definition: The total number of complete buy-sell cycles executed by the strategy over 7 years.

Example: TSLA + SMA_20 generated 72 trades, meaning it bought and sold TSLA 72 times based on the 20-day moving average signals.

Frequency: Ranges from 20 trades (long-term strategies) to 100+ trades (frequent trading strategies).
